Blackened Shrimp Tacos

Description

Blackened shrimp tacos involve a blend of various different cultures including Creole, southern, and hispanic methods of cooking. The true star of this dish is the blackened seasoning. Blackened seasoning consists of a mix of dried herbs and spices that gives meat a charred, brown look. This also provides a delicious, somewhat spicy flavor to die for. Tacos are a perfect way to get to know this flavor as tacos are a great food to mix and match diffrent ingredients to really highlight the power of the blackened seasoning.

Ingredients

For the shrimp tacos

  • 2 pounds of extra-large shrimp (26-30 count), peeled, cleaned, and deveined
  • 2 teaspoons of blackened seasoning
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt, if needed
  • 8 corn or flour tortillas

For the cabbage slaw

  • 2 cups of thinly sliced red cabbage
  • 1/4 cup of mayonnaise
  • 1/2 tablespoon of granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1/2 tablespoon of white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil, avocado oil, or grapeseed oil

For serving

  • 1/2 cup crumbled Mexican queso fresco
  • 1/2 cup of chopped cilantro
  • Lime wedges

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 300°F.
  2. Wrap the tortillas in foil and place them on a baking sheet or pan.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the shrimp, blackening seasoning, and salt.
  4. Bake the foil-wrapped tortillas until they are warmed through, about 10 minutes.
  5. In a large bowl, combine the cabbage, mayo, sugar, black pepper, and white vinegar.
  6. In a large nonstick skillet over medium-high heat, add in the oil. Once hot, add the shrimp.
  7. Cook until the shrimp has slightly browned on the outside and they have cooked through turning pink throughout, flipping halfway through, 2-3 minutes per side.
  8. Place the tortillas in a serving platter or plates. Divide the shrimp and slaw equally between the tortillas.
  9. Garnish with cheese and cilantro. Serve with lime wedges.
