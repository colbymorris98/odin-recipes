Coconut Pound Cake

Description

This is a super simple recipe that you can make for a delicious dessert. You can mix the batter in a single bowl! On top of that, it only takes a few simple ingredients to make. This is a must have dish for those who love coconut and for those who do not like complex dishes. The cake itself offers a moist texture that is great on its own, allowing you to substitute with just powdered sugar if you are not a big coconut fan. An all around great dish that has many more positives than negatives.

Ingredients

For the cake

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, plus more for greasing the pan
  • 1 cup of unsweetened shredded coconut
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 3/4 cup of sour cream, at room temperature
  • 1/4 cup of unsweetened full-fat coconut milk
  • 3 large eggs, at room temperature
  • 1/4 cup of water at room temperature
  • 1 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons of baking powder
  • 1/8 teaspoon kosher salt

For the glaze

  • 1/2 cup of unsweetend shredded coconut
  • 1/4 cup of powdered sugar, plus more for dusting
  • 1/4 teaspoon water, plus more as needed

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F.
  2. Lightly grease a 9 x 5-inch loaf pan with butter and then line it with parchment paper.
  3. In a small, microwave-safe bowl, add the butter.
  4. Microwave for 20 seconds, then in 10-second increments until the butter is melted, stirring after every 10 second increment.
  5. Set aside to cool.
  6. In a blender or food processor, add the shredded coconut and sugar. Blend for 15 seconds until the coconut looks finely ground.
  7. In a large bowl, add the melted butter, blitzed coconut and sugar, sour cream, coconut milk, eggs, and a 1/4 cup of water, and whisk until fully combined.
  8. Use a sifter or fine-mesh sieve over the bowl to sift the flour, baking powder, and salt right on top of the wet ingredients.
  9. Gently whisk until the batter is combined, being careful to not overmix or stir too vigorously.
  10. Use a rubber spatula to scrape the batter into the prepared pan and bake for 50-55 minutes.
  11. Transfer the pan onto a wire rack and let cool for 10 minutes.
  12. Lower the oven temperature to 325°F for the topping.
  13. On a foil-lined baking sheet, evenly spread 1/2 cup of shredded coconut.
  14. Bake it for 3 minutes, stir and spread it, then bake it for another 1-2 minutes until golden brown.
  15. Remove from baking sheet and let cool.
  16. In a small bowl, mix the powdered sugar and 1/4 teaspoon water. Glaze should be runny, so add more water if needed.
  17. Use a pastry brush to brush the top of a cake with the glaze.
  18. Sprinkle toasted coconut evenly over the cake and dust top with more powdered sugar if desired.
  19. Store at room temperature in an airtight container for up to 4-5 days.
