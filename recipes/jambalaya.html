Jambalaya

Description

Jambalaya is a dish made all in one pot that contains rice, meat and/or seafood, and vegetables. Jambalaya is often compared to paella due to its French and Spanish influences. The word 'jambalaya' comes from a Provencal word 'jamabalia' which roughly translates to mishmash. Jambalaya is rooted in the Cajun style of cooking, involving a flavor created by onions, bell peppers, and celery.

Ingredients

  • Peanut Oil
  • Andoullie Sausage
  • Boneless, Skinless Chicken Breasts Cut into 1-inch Pieces
  • Spices and Seasoning
    • Cajun Seasoning
    • Fresh Garlic
    • Red Pepper Flakes
    • Salt
    • Hot Sauce
    • Worcestershire Sauce
    • File Powder
  • Vegetables
    • Onion
    • Green Bell Peppers
    • Celery
  • White Rice
  • Crushed Tomatoes
  • Chicken Broth

Steps

Part 1: Cook the Meat

  1. Season the sausage and chicken pieces with Cajun Seasoning.
  2. Saute the sausage until browned, then remove with a slotted spoon and set aside.
  3. Saute the chicken until lightly browned on all sides.
  4. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside.

Part 2: Cook the Vegetables

  1. In the same pot, saute the onion, celery, bell pepper, and garlic until tender.
  2. Stir in the crushed tomatoes and seasonings.
  3. Add the meat and cook for 10 minutes.

Part 3: Add the Rice

  1. Stir in the rice and chicken broth.
  2. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and cook until all the liquid has been absorbed.
